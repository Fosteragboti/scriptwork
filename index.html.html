About our faculty

About the Faculty

The Faculty of Physical Sciences is one of the key faculties at the University for Development Studies (UDS),
Nyankpala Campus, Tamale. The faculty is dedicated to providing high-quality education, research, and innovation in the physical sciences.

Research and Innovation

The Faculty of Physical Sciences is committed to research and innovation, focusing on areas such as:
1. Renewable energy and sustainability.
2. Materials science and nanotechnology.
3. Environmental science and conservation.
4. Computational modeling and simulation.

Facilities and Resources

The faculty has state-of-the-art facilities, including:
1. Well-equipped laboratories for physics, chemistry, and computer science.
2. Computer centers and IT facilities.
3. Library and research resources.
4. Recreational facilities for students.
The Faculty of Physical Sciences at UDS, Nyankpala Campus, Tamale, is a vibrant community of scholars dedicated to advancing knowledge and innovation in the physical sciences.
With its strong programs, research focus, and facilities, the faculty provides an excellent environment for students to grow and succeed.

Departments

The Faculty of Physical Sciences comprises several departments, including:
1. Department of Physics: Offers programs in physics, applied physics, and related areas.
2. Department of Chemistry: Provides programs in chemistry, applied chemistry, and related fields.
3. Department of Mathematics: Offers programs in mathematics, applied mathematics, and statistics.
4. Department of Computer Science: Provides programs in computer science, information technology, and related areas.
5. Department of Statistics: Offers progams in statistics and acturial science.

Programs and Courses

The Faculty of Physical Sciences offers various undergraduate and graduate programs,
1. Bachelor of Science (BSc) degrees in physics, chemistry, mathematics, and computer science.
2. Master of Science (MSc) and Master of Philosophy (MPhil) degrees in various physical science disciplines.
3. Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degrees in physical sciences.
